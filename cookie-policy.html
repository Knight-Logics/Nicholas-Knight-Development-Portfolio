Cookie Policy

Last updated: May 7, 2026

What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently and to provide information to site owners about how visitors use their sites.

Cookies We Use

Analytics — Google Analytics (GA4)

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors find and use our website. GA4 sets the following cookies:

  • _ga — Distinguishes unique users. Expires after 2 years.
  • _ga_* — Stores session state for the GA4 property. Expires after 2 years.

Analytics data is aggregated and anonymized. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Session Recording — Microsoft Clarity

We use Microsoft Clarity to record user sessions, generate heatmaps, and understand how visitors interact with our pages. Clarity may record mouse movements, clicks, and scrolling behavior. Clarity sets cookies including _clck, _clsk, and CLID.

You can learn more at Microsoft's Privacy Statement. Clarity does not share session recordings with third parties.

Payment Processing — Stripe

When you initiate a checkout on our site, Stripe (our payment processor) sets cookies to manage your checkout session and prevent fraud. These include cookies such as __stripe_mid and __stripe_sid. These cookies are essential for payment processing and cannot be disabled during checkout.

See Stripe's Privacy Policy for details.

Essential Cookies

Our site itself sets minimal cookies for basic functionality (e.g., theme or session preferences if applicable). These are necessary for the site to function and cannot be disabled.

Third-Party Services (No Cookies)

We also use the following services that may make network requests but do not typically set tracking cookies on our pages:

  • Font Awesome — Icon library loaded via CDN (cdnjs.cloudflare.com)
  • Formspree — Receives form submission data but does not set persistent cookies on our pages

Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete existing cookies
  • Block all cookies or only third-party cookies
  • Set preferences for specific sites

Note: Blocking cookies may affect how our site functions, and blocking Stripe cookies will prevent checkout from working.

For browser-specific instructions, visit your browser's help documentation or www.aboutcookies.org.
